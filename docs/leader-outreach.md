# KLUB — Leader Seeding Outreach Plan

*How we populate the leaderboard with 20 real, opt-in traders before mainnet so launch day doesn't look empty.*

---

## The stakes

The leaderboard is our hero feature. If launch day shows a leaderboard with six seeded mock accounts, we look like every fake copy-trading platform that's come before us. If it shows twenty real traders with audited net-of-fees PnL and disclosed risk metrics, we look like the category-defining product we claim to be.

The gap is outreach. There is no shortcut. You have four to eight weeks before mainnet to DM twenty people.

---

## Target profile

We're not hunting whales. We're hunting **legible traders**: people whose PnL, style, and drawdown profile are visible on-chain or on-chain-adjacent, whose accounts have 90+ days of history, and who have a public-facing persona (Twitter, Farcaster, podcast, Discord moderation) to give the handle social weight.

### Style distribution we want at launch

We want variety, not all one style. A leaderboard full of scalpers is boring. A leaderboard with different risk profiles is a product.

| Style | Target count | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Trend follower** | 5 | Highest relatability for retail; longer holding periods; clean chart narratives |
| **Swing / directional** | 5 | The "smart money" story; 1–3 day holds; convicted theses |
| **Scalper** | 3 | Adds a "different animal" data point; shows the board's fee-adjusted ranking works |
| **Basis / funding harvester** | 4 | Low drawdown, "for the yield seekers" cohort; differentiates from vault AUM pitch |
| **Macro / thesis-driven** | 3 | The "read their bio before copying" draw; smallest follower counts, highest depth |
| **Total** | **20** | |

### Acceptance criteria (non-negotiable)

To be listed on the KLUB leaderboard, a trader must:

1. **Have a Bulk or Bulk-equivalent account with ≥ 90 days of history.** (If Bulk mainnet is newer than 90 days at launch, we accept Hyperliquid, GMX, dYdX, or Binance perp histories as pre-launch proof; they migrate to Bulk for the KLUB period.)
2. **Agree to their PnL being displayed net of fees and funding.** Gross-only traders are rejected. We're not negotiating this.
3. **Publish the uncomfortable metrics:** max drawdown, worst month, worst week. If they refuse to, they don't list.
4. **Accept a leader agreement** covering: (a) we display their trades and positions in real time to followers; (b) they cannot pull the leaderboard listing mid-month (only at month boundaries); (c) performance-fee split is 10% platform / 10% leader on mirrored PnL.
5. **Pass a basic vibes check:** no history of rug pulls, no promotion of obviously scammy projects, no affiliations we'd regret being associated with.

### What we don't require

- A minimum account size (we're ranking *quality* not AUM)
- A specific return target (a consistent 6% / month beats a lucky 60% / month)
- Kilobytes of KYC documentation (handled at the ramp layer downstream)

## Where to find them

In rough order of yield-per-hour-spent:

### 1. Your personal network (highest conversion)
People who already know and trust you. Ask: *"who do you know that trades on-chain perps and whose PnL you'd actually copy?"* This is the best source for the first five.

### 2. Farcaster crypto-trader corners
- `/traders` channel
- `/perps` channel
- `/cryptoeconomics` casts that pull in actual practitioners
- Individual casts from established handles: look at who's posting actual trade setups (not just memes)

### 3. Twitter/X (noisier, still worthwhile)
Search: `"my PnL"`, `"trade log"`, `"funding rate trade"`. Filter by engagement from other known traders, not by follower count. Ignore accounts with less than 3 months of posting history.

### 4. Hyperliquid, dYdX, GMX leaderboards
Public leaderboards on competing venues. Take the top 20 of each, filter by whose Twitter is findable. The best ones are also the most-stolen-from — expect a 20% response rate from cold outreach, 60% from warm intros.

### 5. Podcasts & Discord communities
- "Up Only," "The Defiant," "Bell Curve," "Empire," etc. — guests on trading-focused episodes
- Discord servers focused on perps trading (Jupiter's trading channel, Hyperliquid's community, any active trading guild)
- Look for the regulars, not the hosts

### 6. Bulk's own integrator program network
Ask the Bulk team directly: who are their top-20 accounts by realized PnL, and would they facilitate introductions? You're an integrator. This is a fair ask.

**DM them, don't email.** If they have a public email, they'll expect the pitch. If you DM them on the platform they actually use, you're showing you've done the research.

## Sequencing (realistic timeline)

Assuming 8 weeks to mainnet:

| Week | Activity |
|---|---|
| **1–2** | Your personal network + Bulk team intros. Goal: 5 leaders committed (not just contacted). |
| **3–4** | Farcaster + Twitter cold outreach, 5 per day. Goal: 5 more leaders committed. |
| **5–6** | Competing-venue leaderboard outreach. Warm-intro chains. Goal: 5 more. |
| **7** | Podcast / Discord chase for the last 5 slots. |
| **8** | Final onboarding sprint. Every listed leader should have traded at least 5 paper trades in Practice mode before mainnet, so their profile isn't empty on day one. |

**Response rate assumption:** 15% for cold, 50% for warm. So to get 20 commits, expect to send ~80 cold + ~25 warm outreach attempts. That's ~2 DMs per day across 8 weeks, which is achievable for a single founder.

## What to show them

Attach these in the first conversation:

1. **The tearsheet** (`/deck/tearsheet.md`, convert to PDF before sending)
2. **The leader agreement** (one page, plain English — write this before you start outreach; can be pulled from our phase-3.5 legal pack)
3. **A product walkthrough video** (5 minutes, screen-record) — not a deck. Traders hate decks. Show them the leaderboard from the leader's POV: their profile, their tape, the copy-config their followers see.
4. **Testnet invite code** (generate from our `/api/invite` seeded codes — use `klub-0001` through `klub-0020` as founder codes)

## Things that will go wrong

A short list of the predictable failure modes:

- **A leader you commit to blows up on testnet or refuses to publish their drawdown.** Don't compromise. Remove them. We have more slots than quality candidates; empty slots look better than fraud.
- **A "yes" becomes silence for three weeks.** Standard. Follow up once at 5 days, once at 14 days. If no response after that, move on — do not chase.
- **Someone wants to list anonymously.** Okay for the first launch cohort — pseudonymous handles are fine as long as the Bulk account ID is verifiable on-chain. Full anon with no chain-verifiable history is a no.
- **Someone wants a larger cut.** The 10/10 split is our rails. Offer equity or a referral code before you offer a custom fee split. Custom splits are a support nightmare.
- **A leader wants exclusivity.** Politely decline. The KLUB leaderboard is not an exclusive-dealing arrangement; leaders can trade the same strategies on other platforms.

---

## The one thing not to forget

The leaderboard is not a data table — it's a reputational product. The first 20 traders on it signal what kind of klub this is. Pick slowly, pick well.
